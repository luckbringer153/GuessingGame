The Tale of Io

This is an abriged version of the Greek myth of Io. Long ago, Zeus had yet again grown restless and searched the earth for another love of his life. He found Io, a beautiful maiden working in the fields. Zeus flew down to meet with Io and created an enormous storm cloud in an attempt to obscure them from his wife, Hera. However, Hera knew when to be suspicious of her huzband and flew down to punish him. Zeus had just enough time to transform Io into a cow as white as snow in order to protect her from Hera's wrath. When Hera arrived, she could tell that Io was a transformed human; she cajoled Zeus to give her the cow as a present. Once alone with the cow, Hera set her servant, the giant named Argus Panoptes, to guard Io and prevent Zeus from retrieving her. Argus was perfect for the task - his entire body was covered in 100 eyes! By rotating which eyes were "sleeping" (closed), the giant could keep watch from every angle at all times.

Zeus was in a pickle. In desperation, he reached out to Hermes for help, who was happy to assist. To succeed, Hermes planned to approach Argus while disguised as a shephard. He brought his panpipes with him so that he could put the giant to sleep with its music. The next day, Hermes approached Argus the giant...

Hermes must lull all 100 of the giant's eyes to sleep with his music. How many of Argus' eyes were open when Hermes arrived?
